The problem

Manual measure-ups
are pulling your
team off the work
that matters.

Two people. Two to three hours on site. Then more time at the desk resolving dimensions that don’t quite agree — before a single design decision has been made.

01
A resourcing problem
Every hour a graduate or senior designer spends measuring a house is an hour not spent on design, documentation, or a billable client deliverable. A manual measure-up on a typical A&A house ties up two people for half a day — before design has begun.
02
The reconciliation problem
Manual dimensions never perfectly add up. Someone experienced makes an educated guess. If that guess is wrong, the error propagates silently through the design until a builder finds it on site — where it becomes expensive.
03
The return visit
A missed ceiling height. A forgotten junction detail. A stair geometry that wasn’t quite right. Return visits are common, disruptive to the project programme, and require scheduling around the homeowner all over again.

The technology

Leica BLK360 —
precision
in a single visit.

The Leica BLK360 is the industry standard for architectural as-built scanning. It captures a complete spherical scan at ±6mm accuracy — every room, wall, ceiling, floor junction, and external facade — in a single site visit.

Delivered as .RCP and .E57 point cloud files, with an HxDR online viewer link your whole team can access in a browser. Load straight into Revit or AutoCAD and begin.

Why it matters

The scanner
is only half
the picture.

A point cloud is only as useful as the person who captures it. An operator who doesn’t understand buildings will miss the wall junction that matters, skip the ceiling step that changes everything, or fail to scan the roofline detail the heritage planner will ask about.

When RZ Scan attends your site, the person holding the scanner knows what your drawings need to show. Coverage decisions are made with your design process in mind — not just the equipment’s capabilities.

That’s the difference between data and useful data.
